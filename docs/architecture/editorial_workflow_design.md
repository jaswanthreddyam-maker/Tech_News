# Editorial Workflow Design

## Context
Tech News Today requires an explicitly governed lifecycle for its content. Previous iterations conflated the state of an article's *review process* with its *publication state*. To prevent "Scheduled" articles from being implicitly "Approved" (or vice versa), the architecture explicitly separates **Editorial Status** from **Publication Status**.

## 1. Editorial Status
The Editorial Status governs the human or automated review of an article's content, accuracy, and tone.

### States
- **DRAFT**: The article is actively being written or processed. It is not ready for review.
- **REVIEW**: The article is complete and awaits editorial approval.
- **APPROVED**: The article has been verified and cleared for publication.
- **REJECTED**: The article does not meet editorial standards. (This is distinct from ARCHIVED; rejected articles never enter the publication flow).

### Allowed Transitions
- `DRAFT` -> `REVIEW`: Submitting for review.
- `REVIEW` -> `APPROVED`: Editorial acceptance.
- `REVIEW` -> `REJECTED`: Editorial rejection.
- `REJECTED` -> `DRAFT`: Sending back for rewrite/fixes.

### Forbidden Transitions
- `DRAFT` -> `APPROVED` (Must pass through REVIEW).
- `REJECTED` -> `APPROVED` (Must be re-reviewed).

## 2. Publication Status
The Publication Status governs the temporal visibility of the article to the end user. It operates downstream of the Editorial Status. **An article CANNOT hold a Publication Status of SCHEDULED or PUBLISHED unless its Editorial Status is APPROVED.**

### States
- **NULL/None**: The article has no publication state yet.
- **SCHEDULED**: The article is queued to become visible at a specific `scheduled_for` timestamp.
- **PUBLISHED**: The article is live and actively visible.
- **ARCHIVED**: The article has passed its persistence threshold (decayed impact) or was manually retired.

### Allowed Transitions
- `None` -> `SCHEDULED`: Setting a future release date.
- `None` -> `PUBLISHED`: Immediate release.
- `SCHEDULED` -> `PUBLISHED`: Time-triggered release.
- `PUBLISHED` -> `ARCHIVED`: Natural decay or manual retirement.
- `ARCHIVED` -> `PUBLISHED`: Manual resurrection of an old story.

### Forbidden Transitions
- Any transition to `SCHEDULED` or `PUBLISHED` if `editorial_status != APPROVED`.
- `SCHEDULED` -> `ARCHIVED` (Unpublish a scheduled post by setting to None, not Archived).

## 3. Event Flow
In adherence to CQRS principles, state mutations do not simply update the database. They must emit domain events representing the transition. These events enable asynchronous side-effects (e.g., search index updates, notification dispatch, metrics logging).

- `ArticleSubmittedForReview(article_id, story_id, user_id)`
- `ArticleApproved(article_id, story_id, approved_by)`
- `ArticleRejected(article_id, story_id, rejected_by, reason)`
- `ArticleScheduled(article_id, story_id, scheduled_for, scheduled_by)`
- `ArticlePublished(article_id, story_id, published_at)`
- `ArticleArchived(article_id, story_id, archived_at)`

Direct database updates without emitting the corresponding event are strictly prohibited.

## 4. Editorial Intelligence: Coverage Gaps
In RC3.4B, the coverage gap score is calculated as `Demand / CoverageStrength`. 
However, to prevent edge-case explosions (e.g., extremely low `CoverageStrength`), RC3.4C and beyond MUST implement the following normalized formula:

`GapScore = DemandWeight * log(1 + Demand) / log(2 + CoverageStrength)`

## 5. RC3.3B Activation Gate
The activation of the Impact Scoring Engine (RC3.3B) is strictly gated. 
**RC3.3B CANNOT begin unless:**
1. Observation Window is >= 7 Days.
2. The final `Calibration Report` is generated by `CalibrationAnalyticsService`.
3. Scoring weights are formally approved by human review.

Simply completing the time window is insufficient. Human approval of the data-backed weights is required.
